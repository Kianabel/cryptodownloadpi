Bitcoin is a decentralized digital currency that enables peer-to-peer transactions without the need for intermediaries such as banks. It was created by an anonymous person or group of people using the pseudonym Satoshi Nakamoto and was introduced in 2009. Bitcoin transactions are recorded on a public ledger called the blockchain.

Bitcoin is often referred to as a cryptocurrency because it uses cryptographic techniques to secure transactions and control the creation of new units. It has gained popularity as a store of value and a speculative investment, and its price can be highly volatile.
